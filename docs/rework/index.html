Change the Way You Work Forever • by Jason Fried & David Heinemeier Hansson

About This Mind Map

This mind map captures the bold, unconventional business wisdom from Jason Fried and David Heinemeier Hansson, the founders of Basecamp (now 37signals). Rework challenges nearly everything you’ve been taught about business — from the cult of growth to the worship of workaholism, from the necessity of meetings to the importance of formal planning.

Written in short, punchy chapters that read like manifestos, Rework argues that less is more, constraints are advantages, and the best plan is to start working. Whether you’re starting a business or running one, this mind map distills the book’s counterintuitive advice into an accessible format that you can return to again and again.

What you do is what matters, not what you think or say or plan. — Jason Fried & David Heinemeier Hansson

Core Stoic Principles

  • Less Is More: Build half a product, not a half-assed product. Constraints are advantages in disguise. Cut the excess and focus on what matters.
  • Ignore the Real World: The real world is a place where new ideas, foreign concepts, and unfamiliar approaches always lose. Don't listen to it.
  • Meetings Are Toxic: Meetings break your work day into small, incoherent pieces. They drift off-subject, require thorough preparation, and frequently include people who shouldn't be there.
  • Say No by Default: Make each feature work hard to be included. The power of no protects your product from feature bloat and keeps it focused.
  • Workaholism Is Not a Virtue: Working more doesn't mean you care more or get more done. Workaholics create more problems than they solve.
  • Marketing Is Not a Department: Everything you do is marketing. Every email, every word on your website, every interaction with a customer — it's all marketing.
