Why Fossilization Is Rare

Most organisms that die leave no trace. Darwin explains the many requirements for preservation:

  • Rapid burial: Remains must be covered quickly before decay
  • Appropriate sediments: Only certain conditions preserve remains
  • No subsequent destruction: Fossils can be destroyed by heat, pressure, erosion
  • Exposure and discovery: Fossils must reach the surface and be found

The chain of requirements is so demanding that fossilization is the exception, not the rule. Most organisms vanish completely. Most species that ever lived are entirely unknown to us.

On the Absence of Intermediate Varieties

Darwin addresses why transitional forms are so rare:

Why Intermediates Are Rare in the Record

  • Small populations: Transitional varieties likely existed in small, localized populations
  • Brief duration: Transitional phases were probably short compared to stable forms
  • Local change: Evolution may have occurred in isolated areas not being preserved
  • Competition: Transitional forms were likely eliminated by their more specialized descendants

The very process of evolution tends to eliminate intermediates and preserve distinct endpoints.
