Chapter 10: The Blinkit Bet

Quick Commerce as a High-Stakes Strategic Move

The Blinkit deal is presented as a conviction-heavy strategic bet with significant debate around timing, economics, and integration complexity.

This chapter explores how leadership processes controversial decisions: what evidence is used, how dissent is handled, and how commitment is communicated after the call is made.

Decision Under Ambiguity

  • No major strategic bet arrives with complete information.
  • Internal disagreement can improve decision quality when structured well.
  • Speed after decision is as important as the decision itself.

Key Takeaways

  • High-upside moves require tolerance for short-term criticism.
  • Strategic coherence depends on integrating new bets into the core model.
  • Leadership credibility is tested after the announcement, during execution.
