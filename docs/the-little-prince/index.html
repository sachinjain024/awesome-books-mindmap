The Little Prince

A Tale of Meaning and Connection • by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

About This Book

A pilot stranded in the desert meets a little prince who has traveled from a tiny asteroid. Through their conversations, Saint-Exupéry explores what truly matters: seeing with the heart, taming and being responsible for those we love, and the meaning we give to the people and things in our lives.

This mind map presents the essential journey of the little prince—from his planet and his rose, through his encounters with bewildering grown-ups, to his friendship with the fox who teaches him the secret of love. It's a story about what we gain by giving time and attention, and what we lose when we forget to see with the heart.

It is only with the heart that one can see rightly; what is essential is invisible to the eye. — The Fox

THE STORY
Chapter 1
The Narrator's Drawing
The pilot recalls how adults misunderstood his drawing of a boa constrictor digesting an elephant. Grown-ups need explanations; children see with imagination.
Chapter 2
Meeting the Prince
The little prince appears in the Sahara and asks the pilot to draw a sheep. Through simple requests, he opens a world of wonder and questions.
Chapter 3
The Prince's Planet
The prince's asteroid has three volcanoes and a rose. He tends his planet and learns that his rose is unique to him—and that he is responsible for her.
Chapter 4
The Journey Through Asteroids
The prince visits a king, a vain man, a drunkard, a businessman, a lamplighter, and a geographer. Each encounter reveals how adults often miss what matters.
Chapter 5
On Earth & the Fox
On Earth the prince meets a fox who teaches him to "tame" and be tamed—to create bonds that make someone unique in all the world.
Chapter 6
The Snake & Farewell
The prince must return to his rose. The snake offers to send him home. He tells the pilot that the stars will hold his laugh—and that what matters is invisible.

Core Stoic Principles

  • See with the heart: What is essential is invisible to the eye.
  • You are responsible for what you tame: Love creates obligation and meaning.
  • It is the time you gave that makes your rose unique: Relationships gain value through care.
  • Grown-ups forget: Numbers and explanations can replace wonder; we must not forget to see.
