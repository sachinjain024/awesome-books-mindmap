Part Five: The Whistler

The Mayor's Library | A New Source of Words

Death Speaks {.death-narration}

"The mayor's wife had been waiting. Since the book burning, since she saw a girl with singed hands and stolen words, she had been waiting for the book thief to come to her."

"The window was left open deliberately. Ilsa Hermann may have been lost in grief, but she understood something about that girl: she needed books the way other people needed food."

Rosa's Washing

Rosa Hubermann takes in washing and ironing for wealthier families in Molching.

One of her customers is the mayor's wife, Ilsa Hermann.

It is Liesel's job to deliver and collect the laundry.

Liesel has never been invited inside‚Äîuntil now.

Ilsa Hermann {.character-box}

The Mayor's Wife

A woman frozen in grief. Her only son died in the first World War.

She leaves windows open even in winter. She barely speaks.

But she has one treasure: a library. Walls lined with books, floor to ceiling.

And she saw a girl at the burning, a girl hungry for words.

The Library

One day, Ilsa opens the door wider than usual.

Through the halls, up the stairs, to a room that stops Liesel's heart.

Books. Thousands of books. Shelves reaching toward the ceiling.

"You can come here," Ilsa whispers. "Whenever you like. You can read."

The Whistler {.stolen-book}

Liesel begins reading in the mayor's library. Her first choice is The Whistler.

A thriller about a murderer who whistles before he kills.

The library becomes Liesel's second basement.

The Falling Out

War is expensive. The mayor's household must cut costs.

Rosa Hubermann's washing service is terminated.

Liesel is furious‚Äînot about the washing, but about everything.

She returns to the mayor's house and screams at Ilsa.

Through the Window

After the argument, Liesel believes she can never return to the library.

But she cannot stay away from the books. So she finds another way.

The mayor's wife leaves a window open. Always. Even in winter.

Liesel climbs through and steals The Whistler.

Ilsa knows. The window is always open. The invitation never really ended.

Rudy Joins the Theft

Liesel cannot keep her secret from Rudy forever.

Soon he is climbing through the window with her.

Rudy is not interested in reading. He is interested in Liesel. In adventure.

He asks for a kiss as payment. She always says no.

Books for Max {.hope-box}

Liesel begins bringing her stolen books to the basement.

She reads to Max in the candlelight. She shares The Whistler with him.

Max, who painted over Mein Kampf, now receives real books.

Liesel steals for herself. But she also steals for him.

Key Points

  • The Library Opens: Ilsa Hermann invites Liesel into her book-lined room
  • The Whistler: Liesel begins reading in the mayor's house, finding a new world of words
  • Rosa Loses Work: War cuts the washing service; poverty deepens
  • Liesel's Anger: She screams at Ilsa, releasing years of frustration
  • The Open Window: Book thief begins stealing through the deliberately unlocked window
  • Sharing with Max: Stolen words travel to the basement, feeding another hungry reader
