The Changing War

Germany is losing. The news doesn't say so, but everyone knows.

The air raids come more frequently. The rations grow smaller.

Hans Hubermann serves in a unit that cleans up after bombings.

Liesel and Rosa wait. Everyone waits.

The Plane Crash

An Allied plane crashes near Molching. It falls from the sky like a broken bird.

The townspeople rush to see.

Liesel and Rudy arrive before the soldiers.

Inside the wreckage, they find the pilot‚Äîa young man, barely older than them, dying.

Rudy reaches in and takes a small stuffed bear from the pilot's jacket. He places it in the dying man's hands.

Rudy's Struggles

Rudy has been targeted by Nazi officials who want to send him to a special school for elite German youth.

His father refuses, risking everything to keep his son home.

But Rudy is changing. The boy who painted himself black to be Jesse Owens is growing into a young man who questions everything.

He gives teddy bears to enemy pilots. He steals food for his hungry family. He loves a book thief with all his heart.

The Boy Who Loved Liesel

He has asked for one kiss. Just one.

He has asked a thousand times, in a thousand ways.

Liesel always refuses, laughing, pushing him away.

She does not know‚Äîcannot know‚Äîthat she is running out of time to say yes.

Hans Returns

A miracle: Hans Hubermann comes home.

His unit was decimated, but he survived.

He returns thinner, older, his silver eyes dimmed by what he has seen.

Rosa doesn't cry. She holds him at the door. She calls him Saukerl.

Liesel clings to him like he might disappear. He promises: he is home. He will stay.

The Last Days

The war is nearly over, but the bombing continues.

Liesel writes in the basement. Her book is almost finished.

She writes about everyone she loves: Hans, Rosa, Rudy, Max wherever he is.

She does not know she is writing their epitaphs.
