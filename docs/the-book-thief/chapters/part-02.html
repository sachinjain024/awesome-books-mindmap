Best Friend

A boy made of lemon hair and blue eyes, of stolen apples and desperate courage.

He will follow Liesel anywhere.

He asks Liesel for a kiss approximately fourteen thousand times. She always says no. She will regret this forever.

School and Shame

Liesel attends school but is placed with the younger children because she cannot read.

The other students mock her. She beats up one boy, Ludwig Schmeikl.

At home, Hans continues the basement lessons. Letter by letter, Liesel catches up.

The Fuhrer's Birthday {.historical-context}

April 20, 1940. The town gathers to celebrate Hitler's birthday.

Books deemed "un-German" are piled high and set ablaze. Jewish authors. Communist authors.

The Book Burning

Liesel stands with Rosa and Hans, watching the flames consume towers of books.

In the smoldering remains, she spots a book that has survived.

Liesel pulls it from the embers, burning her hands, and hides it beneath her clothes.

The Injury

Liesel's hands are badly burned. Hans notices immediately.

Instead of anger, he treats her wounds and asks to see the book.

They read The Shoulder Shrug together in the basement.

Words as Rebellion

By stealing a book from a Nazi fire, Liesel commits her first conscious act of resistance.

In the ashes of the Fuhrer's birthday celebration, a girl becomes a book thief in earnest.

Key Points

  • Meeting Rudy: Liesel finds a friend who will follow her anywhere, who loves Jesse Owens, who wants a kiss
  • School Shame: Placed with younger children, Liesel fights back with her fists while learning to fight with words
  • The Burning: The Fuhrer's birthday bonfire destroys books, but Liesel rescues one from the flames
  • Ilsa Hermann Watches: The mayor's wife witnesses the theft, setting up a future connection
  • Words as Power: Liesel begins to understand that words can build or destroy worlds
