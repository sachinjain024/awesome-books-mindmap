Part Seven: The Complete Duden Dictionary and Thesaurus

The Parade | Hans's Mistake

Death Speaks {.death-narration}

"They came through Molching in a parade of misery. Pale as paper, thin as wire. They were Jews being marched to Dachau, and they passed right through the town's main street."

"Hans Hubermann's mistake was not kindness. Kindness is never a mistake. His mistake was being kind in public, in Nazi Germany, where kindness to Jews was a crime punishable by death."

The March to Dachau

The concentration camp at Dachau is not far from Molching.

Sometimes, the Jews are marched through town on their way there.

Liesel stands with the crowd. She sees the starving men shuffling past.

She thinks of Max in the basement, hiding, waiting, hoping to never join this parade.

Hans's Compassion {.tragedy-box}

An old Jewish man falls in the street. He cannot get up.

The soldiers are screaming. The crowd is watching.

Hans Hubermann steps forward with a piece of bread.

He gives it to the old man. Helps him stand.

A soldier whips Hans across the face. Six times. Blood runs down his silver eyes.

The Consequences

Hans knows immediately what he has done.

By showing compassion to a Jew, he has invited investigation.

If they search his house, they will find Max.

That night, Max must leave. There is no choice.

The Goodbye

Max leaves in darkness. He and Liesel have only moments together.

Max gives Liesel his notebook‚Äîthe one where he's been working on The Word Shaker.

It is not finished, but it is everything he has.

Liesel watches him disappear into the night.

The Wait

Hans waits for the knock on the door. For the Gestapo to come.

But the knock doesn't come‚Äîat least not in the way he expected.

Instead, Hans receives draft papers. He is being sent to serve in a dangerous unit.

It is a punishment, but not the worst punishment.

Rosa does not cry when he leaves. She tells him to come back.

The Complete Duden Dictionary and Thesaurus {.stolen-book}

Before the disaster, before the parade, Ilsa Hermann gave Liesel a gift.

The Complete Duden Dictionary and Thesaurus

Not stolen through a window, but given freely.

It is a book containing all words. Every word in the German language.

It is the ultimate gift from a woman who understands Liesel's hunger.

Alone

Hans is gone. Max is gone. Liesel and Rosa are alone on Himmel Street.

Liesel throws herself into books and words, trying to fill the empty spaces.

She begins to write her own book. She calls it The Book Thief.

Key Points

  • The March: Jews parade through Molching on their way to Dachau
  • Hans's Act: He gives bread to a fallen Jew and is whipped for his compassion
  • Max Must Leave: The danger forces him back into hiding, alone
  • Hans Is Drafted: Punishment comes as military service in a dangerous unit
  • The Dictionary: Ilsa gives Liesel all the words in the German language
  • Liesel Begins Writing: She starts her own book in Max's notebook
