Part Three: Mein Kampf

A Promise Kept | The Jew in the Basement

Death Speaks {.death-narration}

"Some people said that Max Vandenburg's arrival in Molching was inevitable. Others said it was dangerous, irresponsible, and suicidal. But I say this: it was a result of a promise."

"The consequence of hiding a Jew was not lost on Hans Hubermann... He hid Max anyway. Some promises must be kept."

The Promise

In the First World War, a German soldier named Hans Hubermann served alongside a Jewish man named Erik Vandenburg.

Erik taught Hans to play the accordion. More importantly, Erik saved Hans's life.

On the day Erik died, Hans was spared because he was writing letters for the captain.

Hans visited Erik's widow. He told her: if you ever need anything, find me.

Max Vandenburg

Twenty-two years later, Erik's son Max is hiding in Stuttgart, watching his family get taken away.

He is twenty-four years old, a fist-fighter, a Jew without papers or hope.

His mother gives him a folded paper: an address in Molching. The name of Hans Hubermann.

Max carries a copy of Mein Kampf‚ÄîHitler's book‚Äîbecause nothing looks less suspicious.

Germany, 1940 {.historical-context}

The persecution of Jews has escalated. Kristallnacht happened two years ago.

Jews have been stripped of citizenship, forbidden to work, marked with stars. The camps have begun.

To hide a Jew is to risk everything: arrest, execution, the destruction of your entire family.

Max Vandenburg {.character-box}

The Hidden Jew

A young man made of bones and rage and survival.

He has fist-fought his way through his childhood, been beaten and betrayed and abandoned.

But Max is also an artist. He paints on the pages of Hitler's book, covering hate with images.

In the basement of 33 Himmel Street, he finds something like family.

Arrival

Max arrives at night, starving and half-dead. Hans brings him into the basement.

Rosa, despite her harshness, nurses him back to health.

Liesel is told: there is a man in the basement. You must never speak of him.

The Basement Life

Max cannot go upstairs during the day. The neighbors might see.

He exercises in the dark, doing push-ups and sit-ups to stay sane.

Using the copy of Mein Kampf he carried from Stuttgart, Max paints over Hitler's words with his own images.

This is rebellion in its purest form: taking the Fuhrer's weapon and turning it into something beautiful.

The Seeds of Friendship {.hope-box}

Liesel begins visiting Max in the basement.

At first, she brings him food. Then weather reports‚Äîdescribing the sky he cannot see. Then she brings him words.

They discover they share the same nightmare: a train, a dying family member.

Two people haunted by the same ghosts begin to find peace in each other's company.

Key Points

  • Max Arrives: A Jewish fist-fighter appears at the door, carrying Hitler's book as camouflage
  • Rosa's Choice: The harsh woman shows her hidden courage by accepting the danger
  • Liesel's Secret: A girl learns to keep the most dangerous secret of all
  • Mein Kampf: Max begins transforming Hitler's words into art, hate into beauty
