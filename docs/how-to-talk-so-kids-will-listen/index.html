Core Principles of This Approach

  • Feelings First: Accept and acknowledge feelings before trying to fix problems or change behavior.
  • Respect Goes Both Ways: Children deserve the same respect we give adults.
  • Describe, Don't Evaluate: Say what you see instead of judging with "good" or "bad."
  • Problem-Solve Together: Include children in finding solutions to conflicts.
  • Autonomy Builds Confidence: Let children make choices and learn from experience.
  • Words Matter: The way we talk to children becomes their inner voice.
  • Connection Before Correction: Children cooperate when they feel understood.
  • Progress, Not Perfection: Every positive interaction makes a difference.

Quick Wins to Start Today

  • Replace "Don't..." with "Let's..." or describing what needs to happen
  • Instead of "Good job!" try "I see you..." (describe what they did)
  • When a child is upset, resist the urge to fix it and first listen and name the feeling
  • Give choices whenever possible: "Do you want to wear the red or blue shirt?"
  • Use one word instead of lectures: "Teeth." "Homework." "Jacket."
