Siddhartha

A Journey of Self-Discovery • by Hermann Hesse

About This Book

Hermann Hesse's novel follows Siddhartha from his Brahmin upbringing through asceticism, sensual life, and wealth, to the river and the ferryman. It is a story about finding wisdom not through doctrine but through lived experience and the unity of all things.

This mind map traces Siddhartha's journey from spiritual seeker to worldly man to enlightened ferryman, revealing how wisdom emerges not from teachings alone but from the full embrace of life's experiences—both sacred and profane.

Wisdom cannot be communicated; it must be experienced. — Hermann Hesse

THE JOURNEY
Chapter 1
The Brahmin's Son & the Samanas
Siddhartha leaves his father and joins the ascetics. He masters denial and meditation but realizes that escape from the self is not the same as finding it.
Chapter 2
Gotama & Awakening
Siddhartha meets the Buddha and respects his teaching—but chooses to find his own path. Govinda stays; Siddhartha walks on, awake to the need for direct experience.
Chapter 3
Samsara & the Ferryman
Siddhartha enters the world of sense Kamala, business, gambling. He loses himself until disgust drives him to the river, where the ferryman Vasudeva awaits.
Chapter 4
By the River & Om
At the river, Siddhartha learns to listen. He loses and finds his son, and with Vasudeva hears the river's one sound Om—the perfection of all things.

Core Stoic Principles

  • Experience over doctrine: Wisdom cannot be handed down; it must be lived.
  • Your path is yours: Even the Buddha's teaching is a map, not the territory.
  • The river: Time and the present are one; the river holds all voices and all times.
  • Om: The sound of wholeness; acceptance of what is, and love for the world.
