The Effective Executive

The Definitive Guide to Getting the Right Things Done by Peter F. Drucker

About This Mind Map

Peter Drucker, the father of modern management, wrote this classic in 1967—and it remains remarkably relevant today. The book's central insight: effectiveness can be learned. Intelligence, imagination, and knowledge are essential resources, but only effectiveness converts them into results. This mind map distills Drucker's five practices that transform capable people into effective executives.

The Five Practices of Effectiveness

  • Know Thy Time — Time is the scarcest resource. Record it, manage it, consolidate it into larger blocks for important work.
  • Focus on Contribution — Ask "What can I contribute?" rather than "What do I want to do?" Focus on results, not effort.
  • Build on Strengths — Staff for strength, not absence of weakness. Make the strengths of people productive.
  • First Things First — Concentrate on the few major areas where superior performance will produce outstanding results.
  • Make Effective Decisions — Effective decisions are based on principle. Encourage dissent. Know when not to decide.
