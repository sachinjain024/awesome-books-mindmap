The Cost of Fragmented Attention

  • Shallow work: You can only skim the surface when constantly switching
  • Reduced quality: Deep work requires sustained attention that fragmentation prevents
  • Increased anxiety: Constant task-switching keeps stress hormones elevated
  • Lost creativity: Novel insights emerge from sustained contemplation
  • Attention residue: Each switch leaves mental residue that degrades performance

Building Focus Capacity

Focus is a muscle. It atrophies with disuse and strengthens with practice:

  • Start small: Even 15 minutes of uninterrupted focus is valuable practice
  • Eliminate triggers: Put phone in another room; close unnecessary tabs
  • Schedule focus blocks: Protect time explicitly for deep work
  • Practice meditation: Trains the underlying attention faculty
  • Embrace boredom: Resist the urge to fill every quiet moment
  • Single-task ruthlessly: One thing at a time, completely
