About This Book

The 5 Types of Wealth by Sahil Bloom is a bold reimagining of what it means to live a wealthy life. Bloom — a former private equity professional turned writer and entrepreneur — argues that our culture’s singular fixation on financial wealth has left millions of people materially rich but genuinely poor in the dimensions that matter most: time, relationships, mental health, physical vitality, and financial freedom.

Drawing on philosophy, psychology, science, and personal experience, Bloom introduces a five-dimensional wealth framework that provides a complete map for designing a life of genuine fulfillment. He weaves together insights from ancient Stoics and modern behavioral scientists, from Harvard longevity researchers and Silicon Valley entrepreneurs, into a unified vision of the good life.

What makes this book stand out is its practical orientation. Bloom doesn’t just diagnose the problem — he provides specific, actionable practices and principles for building wealth in each dimension. The book is structured as a design guide, inviting readers to assess where they currently stand in each wealth category and identify the highest-leverage changes they can make.

This mind map distills Bloom’s framework into its essential insights and practices, offering a roadmap for anyone who wants to stop optimizing for the wrong things and start building a life that is genuinely, richly, wholly their own.

The goal is not to be rich. The goal is to be free. — Sahil Bloom

Core Stoic Principles

  • Wealth Is Multidimensional: True wealth encompasses time, relationships, mental health, physical vitality, and financial security. Optimizing for only one dimension at the expense of others leads to a hollow life.
  • Time Is the Master Resource: Money can be earned back. Time cannot. Every decision about how you spend your time is a decision about the shape of your life. Ruthlessly protect and invest your time.
  • Relationships Are the ROI of Life: The Harvard Study of Adult Development — the longest study of happiness ever conducted — found that close relationships, more than money or fame, predict life satisfaction. Invest in people.
  • Your Inner World Creates Your Outer World: Mental wealth — beliefs, mindset, emotional regulation — is the foundation beneath every other type of wealth. Without inner work, external gains crumble.
  • Enough Is a Destination: The endless pursuit of "more" is a trap. Defining your personal "enough" — in money, possessions, and status — is the first step toward genuine freedom and contentment.
  • Design Over Default: Most people live lives designed by default — by society, by employers, by habit. True wealth requires intentionally designing your life rather than passively accepting the one handed to you.
