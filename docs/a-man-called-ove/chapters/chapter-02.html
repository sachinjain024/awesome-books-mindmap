The New Neighbors

A Man Called Ove

Parvaneh and Patrick move in next door with their two young daughters. They back a trailer over Ove's mailbox. They can't drive, can't fix things, and keep asking for help. Ove is furious—but he helps anyway. Parvaneh refuses to be shut out. She sees through his grumpiness and keeps showing up. Slowly, Ove is pulled back into the world.

Parvaneh

Parvaneh is pregnant, stubborn, and kind. She doesn't take no for an answer. She asks Ove to teach her to drive, to help with the girls, to fix things. She also sees that he is sad—and she doesn't leave him alone. Her persistence is a form of love. Ove complains—but he shows up.

"You're the most infuriating person I've ever met. And I'm so glad you're here."
— Parvaneh to Ove (theme of the novel)

Key Insight

Being needed gives Ove a reason to get out of bed. He would never admit it—but fixing things for the neighbors, teaching Parvaneh to drive, and being relied on is saving his life.

Key Takeaways

  • New neighbors can disrupt our isolation—and save us.
  • Being needed is a powerful reason to stay.
  • Persistence and kindness can break through even the grumpiest walls.
