Chapter 2: The New Neighbors

A Man Called Ove

Parvaneh Arrives

Parvaneh and Patrick move in next door with their daughters and immediately disrupt Ove's carefully ordered life. They need help constantly and refuse to be pushed away.

Parvaneh's persistence is practical and emotional. She asks Ove for driving lessons, household help, and support. Ove complains, but he keeps showing up.

Key Insight

Being needed gives Ove a reason to get out of bed. Responsibility becomes a bridge back into life.

Key Takeaways

  • Isolation weakens when others keep reaching out.
  • Helpfulness can reopen connection before words do.
  • Being needed restores dignity and direction.
