Start with Why

How Great Leaders Inspire Everyone to Take Action • by Simon Sinek

About This Mind Map

This mind map presents Simon Sinek’s influential framework for understanding how great leaders and organizations inspire action. At its core, the book introduces the Golden Circle — a simple but powerful model that explains why some leaders and companies are able to inspire while others are not. Sinek draws on examples from Apple, Martin Luther King Jr., the Wright Brothers, and Southwest Airlines to show that people do not buy what you do, they buy why you do it.

Whether you are a leader, entrepreneur, or anyone seeking to inspire others, this mind map breaks down each chapter into accessible sections with key insights, real-world examples, and practical takeaways. The book challenges the conventional approach of starting with what you do, and makes a compelling case for starting with why you do it — because that is what truly moves people to action.

People don't buy what you do; they buy why you do it. — Simon Sinek

Core Stoic Principles

  • The Golden Circle: Every organization operates on three levels — What they do, How they do it, and Why they do it. Inspired leaders start from the inside out, beginning with Why.
  • Manipulation vs. Inspiration: Most companies rely on manipulations (price, promotions, fear) to drive behavior. Only those who start with Why can truly inspire lasting loyalty.
  • Biology of Decision-Making: The Golden Circle maps directly onto the brain. The limbic system (feelings, trust, loyalty) controls decisions, not the rational neocortex.
  • The Law of Diffusion: To reach the mass market, you must first win over the innovators and early adopters who believe what you believe.
  • The Celery Test: When your Why is clear, every decision becomes a simple filter. Only pursue things that are consistent with your beliefs.
  • Why-Types and How-Types: Great movements require the visionary (Why) and the operator (How) working in partnership. Vision without execution is hallucination.
