The Elusive Search for "Bangla Desh"

Part III: Shaking the Centre (1971)

"The creation of Bangladesh was a moment of triumph for India and for the principle that military might cannot indefinitely suppress the will of a people." ‚Äî Ramachandra Guha

The 1971 Bangladesh Liberation War was India's most decisive military victory and Indira Gandhi's finest hour. This chapter tells the story of how East Pakistan became Bangladesh, with India's decisive intervention tipping the balance.

The Crisis in East Pakistan

East Pakistan‚Äîseparated from West Pakistan by a thousand miles of Indian territory‚Äîhad long been treated as a colony. Bengalis were underrepresented in the military and civil service. Their language and culture were marginalized.

The Two Pakistans {.political-analysis}

West Pakistan dominated economically and politically despite East Pakistan having a larger population. Bengali demands for autonomy grew stronger. The Awami League, led by Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, demanded greater provincial powers.

The 1970 Election

In December 1970, Pakistan held its first free national election. The Awami League won decisively, securing a majority in the National Assembly. Sheikh Mujib should have become Prime Minister of all Pakistan.

Democracy Denied {.historical-event}

The military and West Pakistani politicians refused to transfer power. Zulfikar Ali Bhutto, whose PPP won in West Pakistan, would not accept Mujib as Prime Minister. Negotiations collapsed. The military prepared a crackdown.

Operation Searchlight

On March 25, 1971, the Pakistani army launched Operation Searchlight‚Äîa brutal crackdown on the Bengali population. The army targeted intellectuals, students, and Hindus. The scale of killing was genocidal.

The Genocide {.crisis-box}

Estimates of deaths range from 300,000 to 3 million. Rape was used systematically as a weapon of war. Universities were attacked. Hindu villages were targeted. Sheikh Mujib was arrested and taken to West Pakistan. Bangladesh declared independence‚Äîbut had no army to defend it.

The Refugee Crisis

Ten million refugees poured into India‚Äîthe largest refugee crisis since partition. They concentrated in West Bengal, Assam, and Tripura, straining resources and threatening social stability.

Key Statistics:

  • 10M Refugees to India
  • $3M Daily Refugee Cost
  • 9 months Crisis Duration

India could not sustain this burden indefinitely. Intervention became not just a moral choice but an economic necessity.

Indira's Diplomacy

Indira Gandhi toured the world, building support for intervention. She signed a treaty with the Soviet Union that neutralized the American threat to intervene on Pakistan's behalf. She waited for the optimal moment.

Strategic Patience {.key-figure}

Indira resisted pressure for early intervention. She wanted to wait until winter, when Himalayan passes would close, preventing Chinese intervention. She wanted diplomatic cover. She prepared meticulously‚Äîthen struck decisively.

The War

On December 3, 1971, Pakistan attacked Indian airfields in the west‚Äîgiving India the pretext for open intervention. Indian forces, supported by Bengali guerrillas (Mukti Bahini), swept through East Pakistan.

Timeline:

  • December 3 ‚Äî Pakistan Attacks: Pakistani air strikes on Indian bases; India declares war.
  • December 6 ‚Äî Recognition: India recognizes Bangladesh as an independent nation.
  • December 14 ‚Äî Dhaka Surrounded: Indian forces close in on the capital; Pakistani collapse imminent.
  • December 16 ‚Äî Surrender: 93,000 Pakistani soldiers surrender; Bangladesh is liberated.

The Victory

In just 13 days, India achieved total victory. East Pakistan became Bangladesh. 93,000 Pakistani soldiers became prisoners of war. Pakistan was dismembered‚Äîa humiliation from which it never recovered psychologically.

Indira's Triumph {.milestone-box}

Indira Gandhi was at the peak of her power and popularity. She was compared to Durga, the warrior goddess. The victory erased the humiliation of 1962 and established India as the dominant power in South Asia.

The Aftermath

The Simla Agreement of 1972 saw Pakistan accept the new reality. The POWs were returned. The Line of Control in Kashmir was formalized. But lasting peace remained elusive‚ÄîPakistan's military never forgave the humiliation.

Key Takeaways

  • East Pakistan was exploited by West Pakistan despite having more people
  • The 1970 election was won by Awami League but power was not transferred
  • Operation Searchlight killed hundreds of thousands in genocidal violence
  • 10 million refugees flooded into India, creating an unsustainable crisis
  • Indira prepared carefully, then struck decisively in December 1971
  • India's complete victory in 13 days created Bangladesh and established regional dominance
