Be a Kick-Ass Boss Without Losing Your Humanity by Kim Scott

About This Mind Map

Kim Scott learned a crucial leadership lesson at Google: the best bosses both care personally AND challenge directly. This combination—Radical Candor—creates cultures where people thrive. This mind map explores the framework and practical techniques for giving praise and criticism that actually helps people grow.

Core Principles of Radical Candor

  • Care Personally — Bring your whole self to work. Build real human relationships. People aren't resources—they're people.
  • Challenge Directly — Tell people when their work isn't good enough. Clarity is kindness; vagueness is cruelty.
  • Ruinous Empathy — Caring without challenging. The most common failure mode. Feels kind but harms people.
  • Obnoxious Aggression — Challenging without caring. Brutal honesty. At least it's clear, but it damages relationships.
  • Manipulative Insincerity — Neither caring nor challenging. Political, fake. The worst quadrant.
  • Praise Counts — Most people under-praise dramatically. Praise is not optional—it's critical for growth.
  • HIP Criticism — Humble, Helpful, and delivered Immediately in Person (or privately via zoom).
  • Get Before Give — Before you give feedback, create a culture where you receive it. Lead by example.
