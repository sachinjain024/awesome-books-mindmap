Life of Pi

A Story of Survival, Faith, and Storytelling by Yann Martel

About This Mind Map

Pi Patel survives a shipwreck and 227 days on a lifeboat with a Bengal tiger named Richard Parker. Martel's novel explores faith, storytelling, and the will to live—and asks which story we choose to believe when the truth is unbearable.

THE STORY
Ch 1
Author's Note & Pondicherry
Pi grows up in Pondicherry at his family's zoo. He explores Hinduism, Christianity, and Islam—and decides he wants to love God through all of them.
4 Key Concepts
 Ch 2
The Pacific
The ship sinks. Pi finds himself on a lifeboat with a zebra, a hyena, an orangutan—and Richard Parker, the Bengal tiger. The ocean becomes his world.
4 Key Concepts
 Ch 3
The Lifeboat
Pi must survive with Richard Parker. He establishes territory, tames the tiger with the whistle and seasickness, and learns to fish and collect rainwater.
5 Key Concepts
 Ch 4
The Island
Pi and Richard Parker reach a strange island of meerkats and carnivorous algae. Pi realizes it is not safe. He leaves and drifts until he reaches Mexico.
4 Key Concepts
 Ch 5
Mexico & the Story
In Mexico, officials want a report. Pi tells the story with the tiger. They don't believe it—so he tells another story. The question: which story do you prefer?
5 Key Concepts

Core Teachings

  • Faith and doubt — Pi holds multiple faiths; belief does not require a single story.
  • Survival and meaning — The will to live is tied to story, routine, and something beyond the self.
  • Story and truth — We choose which story to believe; the "better" story can be the one that gives life meaning.
  • Richard Parker — The tiger can be read as fear, the self, or the unknown—what we must live with to survive.
