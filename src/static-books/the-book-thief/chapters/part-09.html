Part Nine: The Last Human Stranger

The Crashed Plane | War Comes Close

Death Speaks

"The war was closing in on all sides. I was busy in those days—so many souls to collect, so many colors of sky to record. But I still watched Himmel Street. I knew what was coming."

The Changing War

Germany is losing. The news doesn't say so—the news never says so—but everyone knows. The air raids come more frequently. The rations grow smaller. Young men disappear and don't return.

Hans Hubermann serves in a unit that cleans up after bombings. He is still alive, but barely. His letters home grow shorter.

Liesel and Rosa wait. Everyone waits.

The Plane Crash

An Allied plane crashes near Molching. It falls from the sky like a broken bird, trailing smoke and fire. The townspeople rush to see.

Liesel and Rudy arrive before the soldiers. Inside the wreckage, they find the pilot—a young man, barely older than them, dying in the German fields.

Rudy reaches in and takes a small stuffed bear from the pilot's jacket. He places it in the dying man's hands.

Death Collects

"I collected the pilot's soul while two German children watched. The boy with lemon hair gave him a teddy bear. The girl stood silent, her hand on her friend's shoulder."

"This is what war does: it puts children in fields with dying enemies. It asks them to decide who deserves comfort."

Rudy's Struggles

Rudy has been targeted by Nazi officials who want to send him to a special school for elite German youth. His father refuses, risking everything to keep his son home.

But Rudy is changing. The boy who painted himself black to be Jesse Owens is growing into a young man who questions everything. He gives teddy bears to enemy pilots. He steals food for his hungry family. He loves a book thief with all his heart.

Rudy Steiner

The Boy Who Loved Liesel

He has asked for one kiss. Just one. He has asked a thousand times, in a thousand ways. Liesel always refuses, laughing, pushing him away.

She does not know—cannot know—that she is running out of time to say yes.

Hans Returns

A miracle: Hans Hubermann comes home. His unit was decimated, but he survived. He returns thinner, older, his silver eyes dimmed by what he has seen.

Rosa doesn't cry. She holds him at the door. She calls him Saukerl. Her voice does not break.

Liesel clings to him like he might disappear. He promises: he is home. He will stay.

The Last Days

The war is nearly over, but the bombing continues. Molching waits. Himmel Street waits. Every night, the sirens might sound. Every night might be the last.

Liesel writes in the basement. Her book is almost finished. She writes about everyone she loves: Hans with his accordion, Rosa with her rough love, Rudy with his lemon hair and endless requests for kisses, Max wherever he is.

She does not know she is writing their epitaphs.

Foreshadowing

Death tells us what is coming. He has told us from the beginning. He has warned us that he saw Liesel three times.

The third time, the sky was red.

Key Moments in Part Nine

  • The Plane Crash: Liesel and Rudy witness death up close as an enemy pilot dies
  • Rudy's Compassion: He gives the dying pilot a teddy bear—kindness to an enemy
  • Nazi Pressure: Officials want Rudy for elite schools; his father resists
  • Hans Returns: Against all odds, Liesel's foster father comes home alive
  • Writing the End: Liesel works on her book, not knowing she's recording last days
