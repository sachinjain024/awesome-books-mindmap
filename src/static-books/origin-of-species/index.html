On the Origin of Species

By Means of Natural Selection, or the Preservation of Favoured Races in the Struggle for Life
by Charles Darwin (1859)

About This Mind Map

This mind map presents Charles Darwin's revolutionary work that introduced the scientific theory of evolution by natural selection. Published in 1859 after over 20 years of research, this book fundamentally changed our understanding of life on Earth. Darwin meticulously builds his argument through observations from domestication, nature, geology, and biogeography, addressing objections along the way and culminating in a unified theory of descent with modification.

Core Principles of Darwin's Theory

  • Variation — Individuals within a species differ from one another in countless ways
  • Inheritance — Offspring tend to resemble their parents; variations can be passed down
  • Struggle for Existence — More individuals are born than can survive; resources are limited
  • Natural Selection — Individuals with beneficial variations survive and reproduce more successfully
  • Descent with Modification — Over vast time, small changes accumulate, producing new species
  • Common Ancestry — All life shares a common origin; species are related through branching descent
  • Gradualism — Evolution proceeds through the accumulation of small, successive changes
  • Divergence of Character — Natural selection favors variants that differ most from their ancestors and from each other
