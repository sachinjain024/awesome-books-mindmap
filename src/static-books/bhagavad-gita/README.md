# Bhagavad Gita Mind Map

A visual guide to understanding the 18 chapters of the Bhagavad Gita, based on Swami Prabhupada's "Bhagavad Gita As It Is."

**[View the Mind Map](https://sachinjain024.github.io/bhagavad-gita-mindmap/)**

---

## What This Is

This project provides a **chapter-by-chapter overview** of the Bhagavad Gita to help readers:

- Understand the flow and structure of the Gita before reading
- Get a sense of what each chapter covers
- See key verses and their context
- Navigate the philosophical progression from Chapter 1 to 18

## What This Is NOT

This is **not a replacement for reading the actual book**. The Bhagavad Gita is a profound scripture that rewards deep study, contemplation, and re-reading. This mind map serves as:

- A **preview** before you begin reading
- A **companion** to reference while studying
- A **refresher** after you've read the book
- A **map** to find specific teachings you want to revisit

For the complete experience, read [Bhagavad Gita As It Is](https://vedabase.io/en/library/bg/) by His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada.

## About the Bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita ("Song of God") is a 700-verse conversation between Lord Krishna and the warrior Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. Set within the epic Mahabharata, it addresses life's fundamental questions:

- Who am I?
- What is my purpose?
- How should I act in this world?
- What happens after death?
- What is the nature of God?

The Gita's teachings transcend religious boundaries, offering practical wisdom on duty, action, knowledge, devotion, and self-realization.

## The Three Sections

The Gita can be understood in three sections of six chapters each:

### Chapters 1-6: Karma Yoga (The Path of Action)
Foundation teachings on the soul, detached action, and controlling the mind through meditation.

### Chapters 7-12: Bhakti Yoga (The Path of Devotion)
Krishna reveals His divine nature, opulences, universal form, and the supremacy of devotional service.

### Chapters 13-18: Jnana Yoga (The Path of Knowledge)
Philosophical analysis of matter and spirit, the three modes, and the final teaching of surrender.
