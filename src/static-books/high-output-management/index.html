High Output Management

Practical Management Wisdom by Andrew S. Grove

About This Mind Map

This mind map presents Andy Grove's legendary management handbook, written from his experience building Intel into a global powerhouse. Grove applies his engineering mindset to management, treating it as a production process with measurable outputs and leverage points. This book is a favorite among Silicon Valley leaders for its practical, no-nonsense approach to getting results through people.

Core Concepts of High Output Management

  • Manager's Output Equation — Your output = output of your organization + output of neighboring organizations under your influence.
  • Managerial Leverage — Focus on high-leverage activities that multiply the output of your team.
  • Indicators as Black Boxes — Use indicators to understand what's happening inside your "black box" of operations.
  • Task-Relevant Maturity (TRM) — Match your management style to the employee's experience and confidence with the specific task.
  • Meetings Are Work — Meetings aren't interruptions; they're the medium through which managerial work is performed.
  • One-on-Ones — The most important meeting: subordinate-driven discussions about mutual information exchange.
  • Training Is Leverage — Training your people is the highest-leverage activity you can do as a manager.
  • Dual Reporting — In matrix organizations, use cultural values and shared objectives to coordinate across dotted lines.
